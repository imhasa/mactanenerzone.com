Subdomain takeovers (SDO) are a type of security vulnerability that occurs when an attacker is able to take control of a subdomain of a legitimate website.

This can be done by a number of methods, such as:

  • Exploiting a vulnerability in the subdomain's software.
  • Tricking the subdomain's owner into giving up control of the subdomain.
  • Registering a new subdomain that is similar to the legitimate subdomain.

Once an attacker has taken control of a subdomain, they can use it to a variety of malicious purposes, such as:

  • Phishing attacks.
  • Malware distribution.
  • Denial-of-service attacks.

To protect against subdomain takeovers, it is important to:

  • Keep the software on your subdomains up to date.
  • Be careful about who you give control of your subdomains to.
  • Monitor your subdomains for suspicious activity.

If you believe that your subdomain has been taken over, you should immediately take steps to regain control of it.

This may involve contacting the subdomain's registrar and changing the password.

You should also scan your subdomain for malware and remove any that is found.

Subdomain takeovers can be a serious security threat, but they can be prevented by following the steps above.
